Interpretable Transformers for Human Mobility

IJGIS (under review), 2025 · with C. Chang, D. Ma

This project explores how transformer models can learn structured representations from dense GPS trajectories, enabling accurate and interpretable predictions of travel modes and carbon emissions.

Abstract

We propose a transformer architecture optimized for spatiotemporal trajectory sequences. The model leverages dense embedding layers, a custom speed-attention block, and multi-domain fine-tuning to achieve high cross-city generalization.

Results

On the GeoLife and MOBIS datasets, our model achieves 94–96% accuracy and demonstrates strong transfer learning capabilities. When fine-tuned with only 100 trips, cross-regional accuracy improves by +5.6%.
